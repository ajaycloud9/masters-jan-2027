# Interview Prompts — Admissions & Visa Interview Preparation

---

## Part A: University Admissions Interview Prep

### Prompt I1 — Generate Interview Questions
```
Generate the 15 most common admissions interview questions for an MS in Marketing Analytics / Management Analytics program at a US business school.

Candidate profile: Indian national, MMS Marketing India (9.4 CGPA), brand manager background, transitioning to analytics

For each question, provide:
1. The question
2. What the interviewer is really assessing
3. A strong answer framework (not a scripted answer)
```

### Prompt I2 — Mock Interview Answer Review
```
I am preparing for an admissions interview for [SCHOOL + PROGRAM].

Here is my answer to the question: "[QUESTION]"

My answer: "[PASTE ANSWER]"

Evaluate:
1. Does it directly answer what was asked?
2. Is it specific enough (or too vague)?
3. Does it align with what this program values?
4. Suggested improvements (rewrite the weak parts)
```

### Prompt I3 — "Why Analytics" Answer
```
Help me craft a compelling, authentic 60-second spoken answer to: "Why are you making the switch from marketing to marketing analytics?"

My background: Led 150+ campaigns, then interned at ICICI (90+ UX data improvements), Deloitte (C-suite event data), and Godrej (B2B market analysis). Each role showed me the gap between marketing intuition and data-backed decisions.

The answer should:
- Sound natural and spoken, not like an essay
- Be specific, not generic
- Show self-awareness about the gap and genuine excitement about bridging it
```

## Part B: F-1 Visa Interview Preparation

### Prompt I4 — Generate Visa Interview Questions
```
Generate the 25 most common F-1 student visa interview questions asked by US consular officers in India (Mumbai consulate specifically).

For each question, provide:
1. The question
2. What the officer is evaluating (ties to India, financial ability, genuine student intent, return intent)
3. The key points a strong answer must cover
4. Common mistakes to avoid
```

### Prompt I5 — Visa Answer Review
```
Review my F-1 visa interview answer:

Question: "[VISA QUESTION]"
My answer: "[PASTE ANSWER]"

Evaluate:
1. Does it establish strong ties to India?
2. Does it demonstrate genuine academic intent (not immigration intent)?
3. Is the financial capability communicated clearly?
4. Is it concise (visa answers should be 30–60 seconds)?
5. Rewrite to improve
```

### Prompt I6 — "Why US?" Visa Answer
```
Help me craft a convincing, honest F-1 visa answer to: "Why do you want to study in the United States and not India?"

My situation:
- Completed MMS Marketing in India (SIMSREE, 9.4 CGPA)
- Target: MS in Marketing Analytics (Rochester/Rutgers/NYU)
- These specific programs don't exist in India at this level
- Goal: Gain US work experience through OPT then return to India to apply global knowledge

The answer must:
- Emphasize academic reasons (specific programs unavailable in India)
- Show ties to India (family, career plan to return eventually)
- Not hint at immigration intent
- Be 45–60 seconds when spoken
```

### Prompt I7 — Visa Mock Session
```
Conduct a mock F-1 visa interview with me. Ask me 10 questions one at a time, as if you are a US consular officer at the Mumbai consulate.

After each of my answers, briefly note:
- ✅ What worked
- ⚠️ What raised concerns
- 💡 What to add or change

Start with the first question now.

My background: Applying for F-1 to study MS Marketing Analytics at [SCHOOL], starting January 2027. Tuition funded by [family/loan/scholarship]. Parents live in Mumbai.
```
