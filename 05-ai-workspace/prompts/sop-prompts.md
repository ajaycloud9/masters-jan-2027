# SOP Prompts — AI Writing Assistance

> Use these prompts with any LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot) to draft, refine, and tailor your SOPs.  
> Always provide your resume and the master narrative as context before running these prompts.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation

### Prompt 1.1 — Extract Core Story
```
I am applying to US Master's programs and need help writing my Statement of Purpose.

Here is my background:
[PASTE RESUME HERE]

Based on this, identify:
1. The 3 strongest narrative threads that connect my past to an analytics-focused Master's
2. The single most compelling "inflection point" moment that explains why I'm pivoting toward data-driven marketing
3. What makes my sociology/anthropology background a differentiator, not a liability
```

### Prompt 1.2 — Draft the Inflection Point Paragraph
```
Write a compelling 100-word paragraph that explains why a brand manager with experience at Anika Parekh Consultancy, Deloitte, ICICI Securities, and Godrej realized they needed advanced analytics skills. 

Key facts to weave in:
- Led 150+ campaigns but couldn't quantify long-term brand impact rigorously
- ICICI internship: conducted 90+ UX improvement analysis — first taste of data-driven insight
- Godrej Interio: market-fit analysis for a B2B product — wanted to go deeper into the data

Make it personal, specific, and avoid clichés like "I have always been passionate about..."
```

## Phase 2: School-Specific Drafts

### Prompt 2.1 — Draft Full SOP (School-Specific)
```
Write a 650-word Statement of Purpose for [SCHOOL NAME]'s [PROGRAM NAME].

Candidate background:
[PASTE RESUME]

Core narrative:
[PASTE MASTER NARRATIVE KEY POINTS]

School-specific details to include:
- Why this specific program: [INSERT SPECIFIC REASONS]
- Specific course to mention: [INSERT COURSE]
- Career goal: Marketing Analyst / Consumer Insights Analyst at a US tech or CPG company

Requirements:
- Open with a compelling specific anecdote, not a generic statement
- Show the progression: sociology → brand management → analytics gap → this program
- Be concrete about career goals (short-term: internship in analytics, long-term: analytics leadership)
- Do NOT use the phrases "I have always been passionate" or "I am writing to express my interest"
```

### Prompt 2.2 — Strengthen Weak Paragraphs
```
Here is a paragraph from my Statement of Purpose:

[PASTE PARAGRAPH]

Problems with it:
- [e.g., Too generic / lacks specific evidence / passive voice / unclear goal]

Rewrite it to be more specific, evidence-backed, and compelling. Keep it under 120 words.
```

### Prompt 2.3 — Opening Hook Generator
```
Generate 5 different opening sentences for an SOP for a marketing analytics program. 

Candidate is: a brand manager from Mumbai with sociology roots, who ran 150+ campaigns and wants to transition into data-driven marketing in the US.

Rules:
- Each opener must start with a specific scene, moment, or insight — not a broad claim
- No clichés
- Each should create a different emotional tone: curious, confident, introspective, ambitious, analytical
```

## Phase 3: Review & Refinement

### Prompt 3.1 — Adcom Review Simulation
```
You are an admissions officer at [SCHOOL NAME] reviewing this Statement of Purpose for MS in [PROGRAM]. 

[PASTE FULL SOP DRAFT]

Provide:
1. First impression (1 sentence)
2. Top 3 strengths of this SOP
3. Top 3 weaknesses or red flags
4. The single most important change to make
5. A score out of 10 with brief justification
```

### Prompt 3.2 — Cut to Word Limit
```
This SOP is [X] words. I need to cut it to [Y] words without losing any key arguments or specific evidence. 

[PASTE SOP]

Edit ruthlessly. Remove redundancy, tighten sentences, eliminate filler phrases. Show me the trimmed version.
```

### Prompt 3.3 — Differentiation Check
```
Compare these two SOPs for the same candidate applying to different schools:

SOP 1 (Rochester): [PASTE]
SOP 2 (Rutgers): [PASTE]

Are they sufficiently differentiated? Identify any paragraphs that are identical or near-identical and suggest how to make each uniquely tailored to its school.
```
