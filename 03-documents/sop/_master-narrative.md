# SOP Master Narrative

> This is the core story document. All school-specific SOPs are derived from this. Build this first.  
> Principle: One authentic narrative, tailored packaging per school.

---

## The Core Story Framework

### 1. The Origin — Why Marketing?
*What drew Shradha to marketing? What is the root conviction?*

> **Draft:**
> [Write 2–3 sentences connecting BA in Sociology & Anthropology to understanding human behavior, which naturally led to brand management and marketing strategy. The bridge: understanding people at scale.]

---

### 2. The Journey — What Did I Build?
*Professional experience in a narrative arc — show growth, not just a list.*

> **Arc to write:**
> - Brand Manager at Anika Parekh → led 150+ campaigns, managed cross-functional teams
> - Internships at Godrej, Deloitte, ICICI → progressively data-oriented roles
> - Pattern: Each role exposed a gap — the inability to quantify marketing impact rigorously
> - ICICI Securities: Discovered 90+ UX issues through structured analysis → first taste of data-driven insight

---

### 3. The Inflection Point — Why Analytics?
*The "why now" moment. The realization that brand instinct alone isn't enough.*

> **Draft:**
> [Identify 1 specific moment — a campaign where gut feeling failed, a client question you couldn't answer quantitatively, a data insight that changed a decision. This is the most important paragraph in every SOP.]

---

### 4. The Gap — Why US? Why a Master's?
*What can't you get in India? What specifically does a US MS offer?*

> **Points to develop:**
> - Rigor of US analytics curriculum vs Indian MBA
> - Exposure to US tech/CPG companies that are leaders in marketing analytics
> - Access to a job market where data-driven marketing is the standard, not the exception
> - Peer learning from diverse, global cohort

---

### 5. The Fit — Why This School?
*School-specific section (customize per SOP). Plug in 3 things:*
1. A specific course or faculty that connects to your goal
2. A specific career outcome this school enables
3. Something specific about the program community/culture

---

### 6. The Vision — Where Am I Going?
*Short-term and long-term goals. Be concrete and credible.*

> **Short-term (2–3 years):** Marketing Analyst / Consumer Insights Analyst at a US tech or CPG company, using analytics to drive strategy
> **Long-term (5–7 years):** Director of Marketing Analytics or Head of Consumer Insights, building data-driven marketing functions
> **India connection (optional):** Bridge US marketing analytics practices back to Indian consumer market

---

## Recurring Themes (Weave throughout every SOP)

- **Quantified impact** — 150+ campaigns, 90+ UX improvements, 30% efficiency gains
- **Data hunger** — Pivoting toward analytics from every internship
- **Leadership** — Coordinator, brand management, committee leadership at SIMSREE
- **Sociology/Anthropology root** — Unique lens on consumer behavior (differentiator)

---

## Things to Avoid
- Generic "I have always been passionate about marketing" openers
- Listing internships without narrative connection
- Vague future goals ("I want to contribute to the field")
- Over-explaining the analytics gap without showing you're already closing it

---

## Word Count Targets
- Most SOPs: 500–750 words (strict) or up to 1000 words (if open-ended)
- Every sentence must earn its place
